Programmatic

Data Analysis

It's critical to identify which aspects of the programme are most, and least effective. Knowing this allows us to focus resources on investigating, improving, and iterating upon aspects of the programme that are not as effective.

  • We closely track progress across Specific Literacy Skills
  • We track progress of children in different Grades and/or Stages of reading.
  • We track Dosage and correlations to progress.
  • We constantly launch Iterations of our programme and track their effectivness relative to other versions.

Grade Level Performance

Early Childhood Literacy

The 2-5 year old on our flagship programme made excellent progress in comparison to their peers. They outperformed the control groups by 4X.

Grade R Children

Our Grade R programme is extremely effective, with children progressing twice as fast as their peers.

Grade 1 Children

Our Grade 1 children did not outperform the control groups by as much as the Grade Rs, but they still performed about 20% better. For reference, most academic researchers consider any programme with 10% or greater improvement to be effective enough to warrant scaling.

Grades 2 Children

Our Grade 2 children outperformed their peers by approximately 33%, which is excellent.

Grade 3 Children

Our Grade 3 Children far outperformed their control groups. Moreover, the Grade 3's we took started the year off approximately 30% behind most of their peers, yet ended the year on the same level.

Literacy Skills

Skill Improvement vs Control

It is absolutely critical for us to know which skills we are succeeding in teaching and which ones we are struggling to teach.

First Sound Improvemement

Identifying the first sounds of words is crucial for children because it lays the foundation for phonemic awareness, a key skill that enables them to understand the relationship between sounds and letters, leading to successful reading and spelling.

ECD Phonics

Learning phonics is essential for children as it teaches them how to connect the sounds of a language with letters or groups of letters, thereby equipping them with the skills needed to decode words independently, fostering fluent reading and effective comprehension.
